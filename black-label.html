Portfolio-Level Findings

1. Occupancy Is Concentrated in Smaller Units — Larger Units Are the Drag

The 12 x 40 units lead the facility at 100.0% physical occupancy, while the 14 x 50 units sit at just 44.44%. Of the 15 vacant units, the 14x50 size alone accounts for 5 — more than a third of all vacancy. This isn't a demand problem across the board; it's a product-specific absorption issue with the largest (and most expensive) units.

2. Economic Occupancy Lags Physical by 8.5 Points — Discounting Is Real

At 68.8% physical and 60.3% economic occupancy, Black Label is collecting roughly $13,995/month against a rack-rate potential of $15,525. That's $1,530/month ($18,360/year) in discount leakage. The top 5 discounted tenants alone account for a significant portion of this gap, with some paying $175-$260/month below rack rate.

3. The Facility Has Steadily Climbed From Zero to ~65% Since Opening

Black Label opened in mid-2023 and has been on a gradual lease-up trajectory. Economic occupancy rose from 12.7% in September 2023 to a peak of ~69% in October 2024, then softened through early 2025 before recovering to the mid-60s. The facility hasn't yet hit the 80%+ stabilization threshold that would signal mature operations.

4. Collections Exposure Is Manageable but Concentrated

Total past-due balance is $1,675 across 1 tenant(s). The failed payment log shows 20 tenants with 3+ payment failures — these are the chronic cases that need proactive intervention, not just automated retries.

Operational Implications

Revenue Optimization

  • Review all tenants paying >$50/month below rack rate — especially those on promotional rates that haven't been stepped up
  • The 14x50 and 17x50 units at $530-$650/month may be priced too high for current demand. Consider a limited-time rate to fill the 8 vacant large units
  • Annual rate increases should target tenants who've been in place 12+ months and are paying below standard

Marketing & Leasing

  • 10 people on the waiting list — mostly for 14x30 units (2 available). This signals strong demand for smaller sizes
  • Focus marketing spend on the 14x50 and 17x50 products where vacancy is highest
  • Consider listing large units on RV/boat storage platforms to capture seasonal demand

Risk Management

  • Sheriff Sonko's $1,675 balance with an auction date of 2/19 needs immediate resolution
  • Tenants with 3+ payment failures need direct outreach — auto-retries aren't solving the problem
  • Monitor the 12x40 unit at $190/month (Kenneth Derheim) — that's 54% below rack rate of $410

Data Gaps to Address

  • No move-out reason codes — understanding why tenants leave would inform retention strategy
  • No vacancy days tracking between tenants — critical for calculating true revenue loss per turn
  • Budget vs. actual comparison needs monthly P&L data not currently in these reports

Bottom Line

Black Label is in active lease-up with 68.8% physical occupancy, trending upward from zero at opening 2.5 years ago. The core challenge isn't demand — smaller units have waitlists — it's product-market fit for the larger 14x50 and 17x50 garage condos, which represent 8 of 15 vacant units. Closing the $1,530/month discount gap and filling the large-unit vacancy are the two highest-leverage moves. Together, they represent approximately $73,560/year in incremental revenue potential.
