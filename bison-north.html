Portfolio-Level Findings

1. Facility Has Entered a Structural Decline — Occupancy at Multi-Year Low

Bison North's economic occupancy has dropped from a peak of 82.9% (September 2021) to just 83.0% today. Physical occupancy has fallen from 19/21 units (2020 peak) to 11/20. The 7-year occupancy history shows a clear downtrend since mid-2022 with no sustained recovery. This is the weakest-performing facility in the Blacktail storage portfolio.

2. Deep Legacy Discounts — Revenue Capture at Just 45.9%

The facility collects $1,515/month against a rack-rate potential of $1,825 for occupied units ($3,300 if fully leased). 8 of 11 tenants are paying below rack rate. Danny Short is the most extreme case — paying $100 on a $155 rack rate (35.5% discount) since December 2019, with no security deposit on file. Jonah Ryg at $100 and Mamie Chea at $110 also represent significant discount leakage.

3. Sub-55% Occupancy on Both Unit Types — No Safe Haven

Unlike Bison Central where some unit types are fully leased, Bison North is underperforming across the board. The 20x10x8 units are at 50.00% physical occupancy and the 25x10x8 units are at 60.00%. Neither type is generating enough demand to offset the other's weakness. The facility is too small (20 units) for one unit type to carry the property.

4. Payment Reliability Is a Concern Despite Zero Collections

While no tenants are currently past due, the 222 historical payment failures tell a different story. Mamie Chea leads with 59 failures (Insufficient Funds, Invalid Transaction), followed by Jonah Ryg with closed-account declines. These are tenants already paying deeply discounted rates — the combination of low rent AND payment difficulty signals high churn risk.

5. Cherie Owens Is the Closest to a Concentration Risk

Cherie Owens rents 2 units (Units 4 & 5), paying $315/month — roughly 20.8% of total rent. While not as extreme as Bison Central's Carl Hendershott situation, any single tenant occupying 2 of 11 rented units in a facility this small creates meaningful exposure. Her outstanding balance of $339/unit also requires monitoring.

Operational Implications

Revenue Optimization

  • Danny Short, Jonah Ryg, and Mamie Chea collectively pay $2,100/year below rack — start phased rate increases on these legacy accounts
  • Danny Short has no security deposit — require a $30 deposit at next renewal or rate adjustment
  • The $3,720/year in total discount leakage is significant for a facility generating only $18,180/year
  • Even a 50% closure of rate gaps would add ~$1,860/year in revenue with zero new leases

Marketing & Leasing

  • 9 vacant units out of 20 is crisis-level vacancy — this facility needs active marketing immediately
  • 5 vacant 20x10x8 at $155 and 4 vacant 25x10x8 at $175 — consider introductory rate offers to drive occupancy above 70%
  • Evaluate the competitive landscape: are there newer or cheaper storage options in the area pulling demand?
  • The facility's average length of stay (10.2 months / 8.6 months) suggests seasonal churn — time marketing pushes for spring/summer move-in season

Risk Management

  • Mamie Chea's pattern of insufficient funds declines (59 failures) at $110/month signals high churn probability — prepare for potential vacancy
  • Jonah Ryg's closed-account declines suggest the payment method on file is invalid — get updated card info immediately
  • Cherie Owens' $339 balance per unit needs resolution before it escalates to a collections situation
  • Unit 10 is marked unavailable — determine if it can be returned to service to increase leasable inventory

Strategic Considerations

  • At 55% occupancy and 45.9% revenue capture, evaluate whether this facility's operating costs justify continued operation at current performance
  • The 7-year trend is negative — determine root cause: location, condition, competition, or pricing
  • No waiting list and no recent move-ins suggest demand has dried up — market research is needed
  • Compare per-SF economics with Black Label and Bison Central to determine where capital and marketing spend yields the best ROI

Bottom Line

Bison North is the weakest asset in the Blacktail storage portfolio. At 55.0% physical and 83.0% economic occupancy — with a 7-year downtrend — this facility requires urgent attention. The immediate priority is filling vacancies: 9 empty units out of 20 is not sustainable. The secondary priority is rate normalization for the 8 tenants paying below rack, which could recover $3,720/year. Combined with occupancy improvements from 55% to 75%, the facility could increase monthly revenue from $1,515 to approximately $1,369 — a -10% improvement. Without intervention, the trajectory suggests continued deterioration through 2026.
